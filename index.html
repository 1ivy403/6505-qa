概念与机制类
C
What exactly is "shadow price"? How is it different from the actual carbon tax?
›
Shadow Price ICP Desjardins 2024
1
Shadow price = company's predicted future carbon cost, not today's bill. It includes compliance spending, quota purchase, and internal pricing.
2
Carbon tax = external, mandatory, government-set. Shadow price = internal, voluntary, forward-looking.
3
The key concern: will long-term cash flow survive policy tightening? Will assets become stranded?
Key sentence: "Shadow price is not what you pay today — it's what you expect to pay tomorrow, and that expectation shapes where capital goes."
C
Can you explain the relationship between external carbon pricing and internal carbon pricing?
›
External vs Internal Capital Redistribution CDP & World Bank 2025
1
External: government sets the price (carbon tax or ETS) → mandatory for all market participants.
2
Internal (ICP): company voluntarily sets a virtual carbon price → writes it into project approval → guides internal capital allocation.
3
The logic: external price creates cost signal → company responds with ICP → funds flow from high-carbon to low-carbon projects.
4
Over 1,700 institutions now use ICP globally. BP and Shell both set $40/ton, targeting $100/ton by 2030.
Key sentence: "External pricing forces the question; internal pricing decides the answer — where exactly does the money go inside the company."
C
How do carbon credits fit into this picture? What about quality concerns?
›
Carbon Credits Greenwashing CFC 2024 / Kita Earth 2023
1
Carbon credits = bridge between ICP and external emission reduction projects. When internal cuts aren't enough, companies buy credits to offset residual emissions.
2
Two demand sources in Canada: compliance demand (OBPS system) + voluntary commitment (net-zero targets).
3
Quality varies widely. Must meet: additionality, permanence, third-party verification.
4
As Canada's price hits $80 CAD/ton, companies prefer high-quality credits → carbon credit insurance is emerging.
Key sentence: "The channel matters as much as the price — if credit quality is poor, the capital never actually reaches real emission reduction."

If carbon pricing is economically efficient, why is there so much political opposition in Canada?
›
Political Backlash Provinces Harrison & Schwarz 2024
1
Carbon price increase + inflation → combined effect makes energy visibly more expensive for voters.
2
Although rebate exists, people feel the cost immediately but receive the rebate later — timing mismatch.
3
Provincial economic structures differ hugely — resource-dependent provinces (e.g. Alberta) face disproportionate burden.
4
Conservative Party uses it as a political weapon → lack of cross-party consensus makes policy vulnerable to election cycles.
Key sentence: "Economic efficiency doesn't guarantee political durability. If people feel the cost but can't see the benefit, any carbon price becomes a target."
!
You mentioned policy uncertainty weakens investment. Doesn't that contradict the argument that carbon pricing guides investment?
›
CPU Contradiction? Gavriilidis 2026 / Liu 2026
1
No contradiction — it's a tension, not a paradox. Carbon pricing aims to raise expectations; uncertainty dampens the response.
2
When policy signals are clear and stable → shadow price anchors expectations → investment follows.
3
When signals are blurred (e.g. 2025 benchmark update) → companies apply heavy conservative discounting → investment response weakens.
4
This is exactly why our policy recommendations emphasize signal stability and cross-party consensus.
Key sentence: "Shadow price is the result of two forces — policy pushing expectations up and uncertainty pulling investment response down. The outcome depends on which force wins."
!
Is the Canada Carbon Rebate actually effective? Does it really offset the cost for households?
›
Carbon Rebate Household Impact
1
Official data: rebate can offset or even exceed direct carbon costs for most families.
2
But the problem is perception vs. reality: people feel the price rise at the pump immediately; the rebate comes quarterly.
3
Low-income and rural households may still face net burden due to higher energy dependence.
4
This is why we recommend prioritizing disadvantaged groups and making revenue use more transparent.
Key sentence: "The rebate works on paper, but politics runs on perception. If people can't feel the benefit, the policy loses support."
!
You said ICP doesn't push investment equally. Can you explain the "shielding effect"?
›
ICP Shielding Effect Qin 2023
1
Qin (2023) tested ICP's channels using synthetic control method.
2
Energy intensity = positive mediator: energy-heavy projects respond well to carbon cost signals.
3
R&D spending = shielding effect: R&D absorbs the pressure and "blocks" the signal from reaching other investment types.
4
Implication: shadow price enters the budget, but internal distribution is uneven — not all departments benefit equally.
Key sentence: "ICP opens the door, but R&D acts like a sponge — it absorbs the carbon cost pressure before it reaches other investment channels."

How does Canada's approach compare with EU ETS and China's carbon market?
›
EU ETS China Carbon Market World Bank 2025
1
Canada: hybrid model — federal carbon tax (consumer) + baseline-and-credit (industry) + provincial flexibility.
2
EU ETS: cap-and-trade, market-determined price. Reached €90+/ton early 2026, then fell to €60-70 range. Most mature system globally.
3
China: intensity-based design linked to output — better suited for a growing economy. Price is lower but coverage is expanding.
4
Together, ETS + carbon tax cover about 28% of global GHG emissions.
Key sentence: "Canada chose price certainty (tax), EU chose quantity certainty (cap), China chose flexibility (intensity-based). Different economies, different designs — but the goal is the same: redirect capital."

California IRA CCUS Dilek 2026 / Bistline 2023 / IEA 2023

BP Shell C2ES 2017 / AlliedOffsets 2025

2

政策建议类
R
What are your main policy recommendations?
›
5 Recommendations Harrison 2024 / Rabe 2023 / Jaccard 2024
1
Balance price increase & livelihood: gradual increase, tie revenue to livelihood protection, prioritize low-income groups.
2
Regional flexibility: national minimum + provincial autonomy, differentiated support for resource-dependent regions.
3
Diversified policy portfolio: don't rely on carbon tax alone. Build "pricing + incentive + transition support" framework.
4
Cross-party consensus: legally enshrine carbon pricing to reduce vulnerability to election cycles.
5
Revenue transparency: publicly disclose how carbon revenue is collected, allocated, and spent.
Key sentence: "The answer to the debate is not 'for or against carbon pricing' — it's how to design a system that is both effective and politically sustainable."
R
Is cross-party consensus realistic? How would you actually achieve it?
›
Cross-party Institutionalization Rabe 2023
1
We acknowledge it's difficult — that's exactly the point. Canada's biggest vulnerability is policy depending on which party is in power.
2
Two paths: (a) legal entrenchment — write carbon pricing rules into law so they can't be easily reversed; (b) inter-party consultation — agree on core principles even if details differ.
3
The 2025 decision to cancel consumer fuel charge by the new PM is a real example of what happens without institutional protection.
Key sentence: "If the carbon price can be cancelled by the next election, it's not a policy — it's a temporary arrangement."

The new PM cancelled the consumer carbon tax in 2025. Does that mean the whole system failed?
›
2025 Cancel System Failure?
1
Not entirely — the industrial pricing system (OBPS) still exists. What was cancelled was the consumer-side fuel charge.
2
This actually proves our analysis: political sustainability is the weakest link in carbon pricing design.
3
The benchmark page update in March 2025 shows policy is shifting, not disappearing — focus moved to industrial emissions.
4
For companies, this creates exactly the kind of signal instability we discussed — shadow prices must be reassessed.
Key sentence: "The system didn't fail — it adapted. But the adaptation itself created uncertainty, which is exactly the problem we analyze."
